# Code of Conduct

## Our commitment

This registry is maintained by contributors who share an interest in making Claude more
useful through well-specified, reusable skills. We are committed to keeping this a
welcoming space for everyone who wants to contribute in good faith.

## Expected behavior

- **Be direct and factual.** Review skill content on its technical merits. A comment
  like "the trigger field is too broad because it will activate on unrelated queries"
  is useful. A comment like "this skill is bad" is not.
- **Assume good faith.** Contributors write skills to share something useful. If a skill
  has problems, explain what and why — don't assume the author was careless.
- **Be specific.** Feedback without specifics is noise. Point to the section, the field,
  or the example that needs work, and explain what improvement looks like.
- **Disagree on the work, not the person.** If you disagree with a review decision,
  explain your reasoning. Do not escalate.

## Unacceptable behavior

- Personal attacks, insults, or harassment in any form
- Dismissing contributions without specific, substantive feedback
- Deliberately submitting skills with harmful, misleading, or malicious content
- Misrepresenting tested model compatibility

## Scope

This code of conduct applies to all spaces managed by this project: GitHub issues,
pull requests, discussions, and any other official communication channel.

## Reporting

If you experience or witness behavior that violates this code of conduct, open a private
issue or contact the maintainers directly via GitHub. All reports will be reviewed
and responded to.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may remove comments, close issues, or block contributors who repeatedly
violate these guidelines, at their discretion.
